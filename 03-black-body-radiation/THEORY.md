# Black-Body Energy Radiation

## Core Concept
Black Bodies are physical bodies that hold the capability to absorb 
EM Waves of any wavelength and incident angle. It is very important 
physically because it provides a fundamental understanding of how 
matter emits and absorbs radiation.

The main laws that are used in this project are as follows:

**Stefan-Boltzmann Law** — According to the Stefan-Boltzmann Law, 
the total energy radiated per unit area of the black body per unit 
time is directly proportional to the 4th power of its temperature.

$$M = \varepsilon \sigma T^4$$

**Planck's Law** — Planck's Law describes the spectral radiance of 
EM radiation emitted by a blackbody in thermal equilibrium at a 
given temperature — essentially the full emission spectrum across 
all wavelengths.

$$B(\lambda, T) = \frac{2hc^2}{\lambda^5} \cdot 
\frac{1}{e^{hc/\lambda k_B T} - 1}$$

**Wien's Displacement Law** — According to Wien's Displacement Law, 
the wavelength at which the blackbody emits the most intense 
radiation is inversely proportional to its absolute temperature.

$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$

This simulation applies all three laws to visualize how a blackbody 
radiates energy. Planck's Law gives the full emission spectrum, 
Wien's Displacement Law identifies the peak wavelength, and the 
Stefan-Boltzmann Law gives the total power output. Together they 
provide a complete picture of blackbody radiation behavior across 
temperatures.

## Physical Insight

- The first table tells us the peak wavelength and the region in which the radiation will lie in  this is very fascinating because at about 3000K of Temp the peak wavelength lies at about 966 nm which lies in the infrared region and when the the temperature increases to about 5000K then the region comes to the visible region namely the Yellow-Green region, now at about 8000K the region comes down into the UV region and the case is same with the 12000K 
- A small correlation with astronomy and astrophysics because this is exactly how astronomers determine the temperatures of the stars like for example cooler stars like red giants peak in infrared and appear red, sun-like stars at ~5778K peak at visible yellow-green and appear white and yellow, and hot blue stars at ~10000K peak in UV and appear blue and white.

- The second table tells us the relationship between the temperature and the total power generated by the black body by the Stefan-Boltzmann relation ship
$$M = \varepsilon \sigma T^4$$
- Here we can notice that the relationship between the total power is 4th power of the temperature
therefore we get very high energy
- The actual ratio of power to temperature is-

$$\frac{M_{12000K}}{M_{3000K}} = 
\left(\frac{12000}{3000}\right)^4 = 4^4 = 256$$

- This clearly show the 4x increase in the temperature gives about 256x  increase in the power this is consequnce of the $$T^4$$ relationship given by Stefan-Boltzmann Law.

## Assumptions Made
- Perfect Blackbody (emmisivity = 1)
- Thermal Equilibrium 
- No reflection of the radiations 
- No transmissions of the radiations 
- Isotropic emmision (black body emits radiation equally in all directions)
